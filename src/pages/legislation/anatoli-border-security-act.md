# Preamble
Whereas the State of Anatoli has seen increased threats to its security and sovereignty

Be it therefore declared and enacted by the Parliament of Anatoli as follows

# I. Establishment of the Anatoli Border Services Agency

1. The Anatoli Border Services Agency is established as a body corporate.
2. The Minister of Internal Security shall be responsible for the operation of the Agency.
3. The ABSA shall be responsible for the free flow of persons and goods into the Territory of Anatoli while ensuring the National Security of the State.
4. All Ports of Entry into the Territory shall require Measures of Security which shall be decided by the Agency with a Deadline to be specified by the Agency.

# II. Orders to Leave Anatoli

1. An Allowed to Leave Anatoli is given to individuals who are not permitted to enter into Anatoli, and voluntarily withdraw their intent to enter into the Territory.
    1. An Allowed to Leave may become a Deportation Order if the concerned individual is found to remain within the territory.
2. A Deportation Order is an order given to individuals who are not permitted to enter Anatoli and are indefinitely banned from entering into the Territory upon receipt of the Order.

# III. Conditions on the Refusal of Entry

1. A Person may be denied entry into the Territory based on:
    1. Missing or incomplete identification
    2. A prior conviction or "no contest' to a criminal offence under domestic or international law for which a pardon is not granted
    3. Insufficient evidence of the ability for the Person to sustain themselves while remaining within the Territory
    4. Insufficient evidence that the Person is authorized to enter the territory
    5. Any conflict of interest as may be defined in any other Act of Parliament
    6. Any other reason in the interest of public safety
    7. Whether the Person was previously refused entry for any reason stated in sections (a) to (f)

# IV. Entrance Responsibility

1. Every entrant shall be responsible for properly declaring and reporting their entrance at an Anatoli port of entry.
2. Every entrant who enters Anatoli without due authorization and as such knowingly remains in the territory is liable to, upon discovery:
    1. An immediate deportation order from the State;
    2. Monetary penalties; or;
    3. Criminal charges under the Criminal Code of Anatoli.

# V. Exit Responsibility

1. If an entrant is denied entry into the territory under the previous part, and is denied entry at an air-based port of entry, it shall be the responsibility of the originating airline to remove the person from the territory without delay.

# VI. Secure Import and Export of Goods

1. A Consignment entering into the Territory must contain an accurate, itemized declaration of items in the consignment.
2. Any declarative material such as material containing vital package delivery information and addresses, such as package delivery flags, address books/sheets, and the itemized declaration list itself, do not need to be declared.
3. The itemized declaration list must contain a signed statement attesting that said list is true and accurate, as well as a declaration of dangerous goods.

## 1. Control of Hazardous Materials

1. Hazardous materials cannot enter and be transported through the territory without due authorization from the State.
    1. Hazardous materials may be declared by an order of the Sovereign-in-council.
2. Hazardous materials must be declared in an itemized declarations list, and must be shipped with a valid license for said material.
3. Everyone who, without proper authorization, knowingly attempts to export a controlled or hazardous material is guilty of
    1. An indictable offence and is liable to
        1. A monetary penalty not exceeding 400% of the actual value of the consignment;
        2. A prison term not longer than four (4) years; or;
        3. Both punishments.
    2. An offence punishable by summary conviction.

## 2. Refusal and Seizure of Goods

1. The ABSA reserves the right to refuse entry of any imported goods for entry provided that
    1. It contains an itemized declaration list that is inaccurate;
    2. It contains any undeclared hazardous materials; or;
    3. It may pose a public safety or national security threat
2. An item refused for entry under subsection (a) shall be returned to the importer
    1. If a consignment is marked as ‘AB’, it must be safely disposed of.
3. An item refused for entry under subsections b) or c) is considered seized and must be safely disposed of, regardless of sender instructions.
4. Everyone commits an offence who, knowing that a package contains items posing a grave danger to public safety, clears it for entry.
5. Everyone who commits an offence under the previous is guilty of an indictable offence and is liable to a prison term not less than 5 years.
